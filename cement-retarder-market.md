<p><p>The cement retarder market is primarily divided into two categories: organic agents and inorganic agents. Organic agents, derived from natural or synthetic sources, are commonly used to delay the setting time of cement mixtures, enabling better workability and longer application periods. In contrast, inorganic agents, typically mineral-based compounds, also serve to prolong the setting time but may operate through different chemical mechanisms. Both types are essential in construction, facilitating the use of concrete in various environmental conditions and applications.</p></p>
