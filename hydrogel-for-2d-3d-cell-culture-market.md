<p><p>The hydrogel market for 2D and 3D cell culture is divided into two main types: natural and synthetic hydrogels. Natural hydrogels, derived from biological sources like collagen or alginate, provide a supportive environment that closely mimics the extracellular matrix, promoting cell adhesion and growth. Synthetic hydrogels, engineered from polymers, offer customizable properties such as stiffness and degradability, allowing for tailored applications. Both types are crucial for advancing research in tissue engineering, drug testing, and regenerative medicine.</p></p>
