<p><p>The edible plant-based oils market is categorized into two primary types: herbaceous oils and woody plant oils. Herbaceous oils are derived from non-woody plants, such as seeds and nuts, and are known for their light flavors and high nutritional value. Examples include olive, sunflower, and canola oils. In contrast, woody plant oils come from trees and shrubs, offering richer flavors and more distinct aromas. Popular examples include coconut and palm oils, often used for cooking and baking, as well as in cosmetics.</p></p>
