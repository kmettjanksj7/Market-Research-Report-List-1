<p><p>Laminated Veneer Lumber (LVL) is an engineered wood product made from thin layers of wood veneers bonded together with adhesives, offering high strength and stability. The LVL market is divided into two primary types: structural and non-structural. Structural LVL is utilized in load-bearing applications such as beams, headers, and joists, providing critical support in construction. Non-structural LVL, on the other hand, is used primarily for aesthetic purposes or in non-load-bearing applications, such as interior finishes and cabinetry.</p></p>
