<p><p>The railway battery market features several key types: Lead-Acid, Li-Ion, and Ni-Cd batteries. Lead-Acid batteries are cost-effective and widely used for their reliability in powering signaling and emergency systems. Li-Ion batteries offer higher energy density and longer lifespans, making them ideal for modern electric trains. Ni-Cd batteries, known for their durability and performance under extreme temperatures, are used in specific applications, such as backup power. Each type serves distinct needs in the evolving railway industry.</p></p>
