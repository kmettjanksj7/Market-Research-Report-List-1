<p><p>The low light sensors market is primarily divided into two types: analog and digital sensors. Analog low light sensors provide continuous output signals that vary based on the intensity of light detected, making them ideal for applications requiring smooth, real-time responses. In contrast, digital low light sensors convert light levels into discrete values, enabling easier integration with digital systems and precise data processing. Both types serve diverse applications, including security systems, wildlife monitoring, and automated lighting controls.</p></p>
