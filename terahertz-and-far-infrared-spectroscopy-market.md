<p><p>Terahertz and Far-infrared spectroscopy markets encompass various types of infrared spectroscopy: near-infrared, mid-infrared, and far-infrared. Near-infrared spectroscopy focuses on the 0.75 to 1.5 μm wavelength range, ideal for analyzing organic compounds and moisture content. Mid-infrared spectroscopy, spanning 2.5 to 25 μm, is effective for identifying molecular vibrations and chemical bonding. Far-infrared spectroscopy, covering wavelengths from 25 μm to 1 mm, is primarily used for studying low-frequency molecular motions and solid-state properties, benefiting material science and biology applications.</p></p>
