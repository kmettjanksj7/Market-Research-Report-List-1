<p><p>The calcium carbonate market is primarily divided into two types: Ground Calcium Carbonate (GCC) and Precipitated Calcium Carbonate (PCC). GCC is produced by grinding limestone or marble, resulting in a coarse powder used in applications like construction, paper, and plastics for its filler properties. In contrast, PCC is created through a chemical process, resulting in finer, purer particles with diverse uses in industries such as pharmaceuticals, food, and cosmetics for its controlled particle size and surface area.</p></p>
