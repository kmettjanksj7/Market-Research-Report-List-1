<p><ul><li>Digital Microfluidic Chip PCR</li><li>Droplet Digital PCR</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Digital PCR (dPCR) is a highly sensitive molecular technique that provides precise nucleic acid quantification by partitioning a sample into thousands of individual reactions. Two key market types are Digital Microfluidic Chip PCR and Droplet Digital PCR. Digital Microfluidic Chip PCR employs miniaturized chips to manipulate fluids, enhancing throughput and efficiency. In contrast, Droplet Digital PCR uses microdroplets to isolate and amplify DNA fragments, allowing for precise measurements of target sequences. Both methods are pivotal in research, diagnostics, and personalized medicine.</p></p>
